a naturally brilliant student, instead I think everything I've ever done in my life stems from hard work and
learning from (many) failures. Because of this background I've really grown to enjoy teaching and explaining complicated

the intersection of mathematics and computer science and try to learn more about it every day. I'm especially interested in probability
theory and intend on taking a series of classes pertaining to that field in the coming semesters. Currently I lab assist for CS61A.
I'm involved with Blockchain at Berkeley, Cal Hiking and Outdoor Society, and Cal Club Soccer outside of academic work. If you've read any cool papers
on math, physics, quantum computing, or especially dank memes lately feel free to send them to me via any of these.
